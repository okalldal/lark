  **SHARED checkout** (it's a plain `Task`, not `isolation: worktree`). The #549 reviewer
  *reproduced the teeth-check locally* — `git checkout` of the sprint branch, injected a
  corruption, ran tests, then `git checkout master` — and **left the orchestrator's shared
  checkout on `master`** with a stray `lark_proc/` artifact. The orchestrator had to detect
  this (a spurious "CLAUDE.md modified" reminder, actually just master's older copy) and

  `isolation: worktree`; or (b) forbid review sub-agents from any working-tree mutation

  re-asserts its branch after every review. The §5 brief was amended mid-sprint for #550 to
  explicitly forbid checkouts, which worked. Suggest codifying (b) in §5 of start-sprint.md.

  brief already carries a strongly-worded anti-stranding clause and the worker still tripped
  it — suggests the clause needs to be even more prominent, or the harness should refuse to
  end a worker's turn while it has live background children. The worker also reported its
  background reviewers' output files "weren't readable from the documented path," forcing a
  fresh foreground reviewer — worth investigating as a separate harness gap.
